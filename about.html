About The App

What This Country Intelligence Tool Does

This app is built to help users explore countries visually, compare them with real public data, and make more informed relocation, safety, opportunity, and long-term planning decisions.

Why It Is Useful

This app is designed to help users move from curiosity to decision-making much faster.

For relocation planning

Users can explore whether a country is good for a family, a retiree, a student, or a career-focused move.

For fast country understanding

Instead of reading many different websites, users get a condensed visual overview in one place.

For comparing tradeoffs

Some countries are safer, others cheaper, others better for jobs. The app makes those tradeoffs visible.

For data-backed exploration

The experience is built around public indicators, not made-up country scores.

How To Use The App Well

The experience is strongest when users move from broad exploration to focused decision-making.

Start with the map

Use map layers and filters first to narrow the world down into a shortlist that matches your goals.

Switch the decision mode

Change between family, career, retirement, study, remote work, and investment modes to see how country rankings shift.

Go deeper on a country

Click a country to inspect story mode, hazards, visa pathway, job fit, live weather, crime trend, and alerts in one place.

Data and Trust Notes

The app tries to stay transparent about what is direct data and what is modeled interpretation.

Direct public indicators

  • World Bank for GDP, inflation, poverty, inequality, unemployment, tourism, and more
  • WHO for health-related indicators
  • UN SDG API for crime trend data
  • ND-GAIN and EM-DAT for climate vulnerability and disaster pressure
  • REST Countries and OpenFlights for metadata, airlines, and airports

Modeled layers and lenses

  • Decision Fit, Family Safety, and Job Fit are composite scores built from real indicators
  • These are meant to help interpretation, not replace official legal or immigration advice
  • Users should still verify city-level realities, visa rules, and local cost conditions before acting
